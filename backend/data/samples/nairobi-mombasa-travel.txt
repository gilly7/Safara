Kenya Practical Travel Information

Best Visiting Seasons
- Safari (interior): June–October (dry, best wildlife), January–February (calving in Mara)
- Coast (Mombasa, Diani, Lamu): December–March warm and dry; April–May can be rainy
- Mount Kenya trekking: January–February, August–September

Food & Culture
Kenyan cuisine blends African, Indian, and Arab influences. Try nyama choma (grilled meat), ugali, sukuma wiki, and coastal dishes like biryani and samaki wa kupaka. Respect local customs: dress modestly in rural areas and when visiting mosques. Swahili greetings: "Jambo" (hello), "Asante" (thank you).

Getting Around
- Domestic flights connect Nairobi to Maasai Mara, Mombasa, Lamu, and other hubs
- Safari tours typically use 4x4 vehicles; self-drive possible in some parks with 4x4
- Nairobi to Mombasa: flight (~1 hr) or SGR train (~5 hrs) or road (~8 hrs)

Currency
Kenyan Shilling (KES). ATMs in cities; carry cash for rural areas and park fees.

Health
Consult a travel clinic for vaccinations. Malaria risk in coastal and lowland areas — use repellent and prophylaxis as advised. Tap water not recommended; use bottled or filtered water.

Coastal Highlights — Diani Beach
Diani offers kite surfing, snorkeling, and colobus monkey sanctuary visits. Luxury and mid-range resorts available. 30 km south of Mombasa via ferry and road.

Lamu Travel Tips
Lamu Old Town is pedestrian-only. Respect Ramadan customs if visiting during the holy month. Best explored by dhow and on foot.

Great Migration Facts
The migration is driven by rainfall and grass growth. River crossings (Mara River) are dramatic but timing varies yearly. Book lodges 6–12 months ahead for peak season.
