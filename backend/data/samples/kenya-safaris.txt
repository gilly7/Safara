Kenya Safari Guide — Key Destinations

Maasai Mara National Reserve
The Maasai Mara is Kenya's most famous safari destination, located in the Great Rift Valley. It is renowned for the Great Migration (July–October), when over 1.5 million wildebeest and zebras cross from the Serengeti. The Big Five (lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, rhino) are commonly sighted. Best visiting season: dry months June–October for migration; January–February for calving season.

Amboseli National Park
Amboseli lies at the foot of Mount Kilimanjaro and is famous for large elephant herds and stunning mountain views. Dry season (June–October) offers the clearest Kilimanjaro views. The park is approximately 240 km from Nairobi (4–5 hours by road).

Tsavo National Parks (East & West)
Tsavo is Kenya's largest national park, divided into Tsavo East and Tsavo West. Known for red elephants (dust-coated), Mzima Springs, and diverse landscapes. Less crowded than Maasai Mara. Best season: dry months for wildlife viewing.

Lake Nakuru National Park
Famous for flamingos (numbers vary seasonally), rhino sanctuary, and acacia woodlands. Located 160 km northwest of Nairobi. Good year-round; flamingo numbers peak in dry season.

Nairobi
Kenya's capital. Key attractions: Nairobi National Park (wildlife within city limits), Giraffe Centre, Karen Blixen Museum, and National Museum. Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (NBO) is the main international gateway.

Mombasa & Diani Beach
Mombasa is Kenya's coastal hub with Swahili culture, Fort Jesus UNESCO site, and beaches. Diani Beach (south of Mombasa) offers white sand, coral reefs, and water sports. Best beach season: December–March (dry, warm).

Lamu Archipelago
Historic Swahili town, UNESCO World Heritage Site. Car-free island with dhow sailing and traditional architecture. Accessible by flight from Nairobi or Mombasa.

Mount Kenya
Africa's second-highest peak. Trekking routes include Sirimon, Naro Moru, and Chogoria. Best climbing seasons: January–February and August–September.

Visa & Entry
Most visitors need an e-visa (apply at evisa.go.ke). Passport valid 6+ months. Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from endemic countries.

Safety Tips
Use registered tour operators. Avoid walking alone at night in cities. Keep valuables secure. Drink bottled water. Malaria prophylaxis recommended for safari regions.

Sustainable Tourism
Choose eco-certified lodges. Respect wildlife viewing distances. Support community conservancies. Do not litter in parks.
